WMLAlive: The Body as an Island

Solo Exhibition · 2023.11.17-12.03 · spaceuooyoung · Supported by Seoul Foundation for Arts and Culture(SFAC)

’The Island of Body:WMLAlive' explores the relationship between the body and presence through the question 'What Makes Life Alive?' 'The Island of Body' begins with an interest in each person's individual experiences and grounds for determining that 'what was thought to be alive is dead' or 'what was thought to be dead is alive,' as well as the process that triggers these judgments. At the boundary between the living and the non-living, I ask what grounds lead us to think 'that body' is alive, not alive, or has never been alive at all. This exhibition is an attempt to discuss the body as evidence of existence and what it means to be alive, through the spectrality of inanimate objects.

  • One Breath (2023)
    Arduino UNO R3, Distance sensors, 5V Air motor, Solenoid Valve, LED lights 12v, silver blanket, Steel, Mirror Film, Wire Extension | 160 × 40 × 40(cm) Inspired by a chance conversation with a viewing stone collector, this work examines how the human act of discovering forms in nature—a 'universe of meaninglessness'—and attributing meaning to them, with all its arbitrariness, can serve as evidence of being alive. Through the breath that maintains homeostasis in living organisms and a thin membrane whose transparency shifts with light, the work invites viewers to contemplate what it means to be alive through interactive engagement with an inanimate object's outer skin.
  • Star (2023)
    Ceramic, Vinyl, Wire | 230 × 320 × 320 cm Taking inspiration from Park Lee-So's Three Star Show (1994), Byeolgut (2023) stems from the linguistic play of "byeolgut anin ge" (nothing special). This work invites viewers to stop making hasty judgments about whether someone or something is "special" without truly knowing, and instead to look carefully. A small ceramic "byeolgut" object is placed inside a transparent tetrapod air sculpture, visible only to those who approach closely.
  • Machine Writing Eternity: Prototype (2023)
    Arduino Uno R3, Servo motors, Acrylic, paper, waterbrush, water | 28 × 15 × 25 cm The Machine That Writes Young-Won (01) (2023) extends the 2018 VR art game Young-Young, Pointless Sight. While the earlier work depicted infinite regression from 'absence' to 'absence' by examining how the body is re-sensed after visual erasure, this piece seeks movement from 'absence' to 'presence' through performativity. Addressing "What defines being alive?", the artist emphasizes the performativity of living. Like the song lyric "The only eternal thing is the word 'eternal,'" nothing is eternal in cyclical nature and all things fade—yet the very act of striving to continue doing something can be evidence of being alive. Using vaporizing material, the work repeatedly inscribes "young-won (eternal-origin) (01)," articulating an 'eternity that is not eternal.'

  • Workshop: WMLAlive X Fern Soul Dance Club
    June 25, 2023 (Sun) 14:00-17:00 Together with Gosari Dance Club, which pursues dance that anyone can enjoy anywhere—even while practicing briefly during traffic light waits in daily life—we examine scenes of objects encountered in everyday life and memorable moments through dancing to techno music. Through walking, repetition, and stillness, we sense 'what it might be to be in a state of being alive.'Participants engage in embodied practices that question the boundaries between living and non-living matter.
  • Workshop: Circuit Making Memories
    Nov 17, 2023 (Fri) 14:00-16:00 Exploring the intersection of technology, materiality, and remembrance. We create a circuit that remembers and forgets 1 bit through NOT, AND, and OR logic gates, using Memory—the component responsible for storage in computers. After the process, we think together about the constituent elements of memory.

Suh Youri discusses [the planning background and production process of 'The Island Called Body'], Lim Heeju speaks about [experiential concerns regarding making sounds], and Haemin Hae talks about [methods of coating breathing things with transparent membranes].
