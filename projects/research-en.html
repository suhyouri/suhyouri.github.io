the Walking Methods of Inanimate Objects

Research · 2025 · Seongnam Cultural Foundation — Young Artist Support Grant

While creating walking inanimate objects, I reverse-engineer the prerequisite actions required for 'walking' to be performed. I researched mechanisms to implement previously planned works such as a 'walking mirror' and a 'walking beanbag,' and produced a prototype of the walking mirror. As a follow-up to the kinetic research project conducted in 2024, this is a research focused on methods for 'safe walking.'

  • Walking Mirror (2025)
    Steel, Acrylic mirror, LiDAR sensor, Mecanum wheels, Arduino Mega, Feather32uF, PLA, OLED, Battery pack A work created by imagining what would happen if a very familiar object had a mind and could move.
    1. Lightweighting of the exterior and mirror material
    2. Enhanced safety through addition of a LiDAR sensor
    3. Secured freedom of movement with Mecanum wheels
    4. Switched from Bluetooth to Feather32UF RF69 for 1:1 paired communication to ensure communication reliability
    5. Added a facial emotion recognition system via Raspberry Pi
    6. Added real-time speed conversion control functionality
